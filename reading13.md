# Diversity & Inclusion in the Tech Industry

## **Consider the History:** [That Time When Women Stopped Coding](https://www.npr.org/sections/money/2014/10/21/357629765/when-women-stopped-coding)

* What occured during the same time as the beginning of the decline of women in computer sciene?

  * The share of women in computer science started falling at roughly the same moment when personal computers started showing up in U.S. homes in significant numbers. These early personal computers weren't much more than toys. You could play pong or simple shooting games, maybe do some word processing. And these toys were marketed almost entirely to men and boys.

* Why does it matter that males had been playing on computers growing up?
  
  * It allowed men to be ahead of women when it came to computers because they had grown up with computers

## **Ask the Question** [Why Diversity Matters to Your Tech Company](https://www.usatoday.com/story/tech/columnist/2015/07/21/why-diversity-matters-your-tech-company/30419871/)

* When are diversity efforts most successful?

  * Most successful when they are driven by commitment from company leaders. Meaningful commitment requires leaders to understand why diversity matters

* Why do diverse companies perform better?

  * Studies show that diverse companies are smarter and more creative. Diversity brings different ways of seeing different problems and thus allows for faster and better ways to solving those problems.

* Give an example of how a diverse company can serve a diverse user base or vise-versa.

  * When the employees of an organization better represent their users and desired users, they will build more effectively for those groups.
